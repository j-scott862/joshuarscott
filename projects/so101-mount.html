SO-101 Jetson Orin Mount

Designed and fabricated a modular 3D-printed mounting system that enables an SO-101 robotic arm to operate with an onboard Jetson Orin and battery pack—removing the need for a tethered laptop or external power source.

Problem context

Typical SO-101 configurations rely on externally connected compute hardware, which limits portability, repeatability, and deployment flexibility during experimentation.

Design approach

  • Modular architecture: the mount was designed as three independent components.
  • Targeted iteration: each component is exported separately, enabling selective reprints without redesigning the entire system.
  • Fitment-first: geometry was designed around mechanical constraints and spatial integration on the arm.

Outcome

  • Enabled an untethered configuration with onboard compute + battery pack.
  • Improved setup portability for testing and demonstrations.
  • Reduced friction for iteration by decoupling parts and reprint cycles.

Next iteration

  • Weight reduction and stiffness tuning for improved dynamics.
  • Cable routing and strain relief improvements.
  • Quick-release or standardized mounting points for faster swaps.
